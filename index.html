About Me

My journey began in Northern Virginia, though growing up in a military family meant my early years were defined by constant movement across the United States. Throughout these changes, two things remained constant: a profound connection to animals and a fascination with how the world is structured.

While I initially expected to become a veterinarian even shadowing at clinics as a child my path took a detour through the arts. I spent nine years as a saxophonist, earning various honors and intending to pursue music professionally. However, during my time at James Madison University, I discovered a "hidden beauty" in Mathematics and Statistics. My experience in Linear Algebra was a turning point; it transformed math from a subject I simply enjoyed into a lifelong calling.

After spending time as a Software and DevOps Engineer, I realized that my true passion lies in the intersection of theory and application. I have since presented research both within and outside of JMU, and I am now dedicated to bridging my two worlds: Mathematics and Animal Science. I believe that quantitative rigor can unlock new insights into the biological world I’ve loved since childhood.

When I’m not deep in a proof or researching, you can find me tutoring students in music and math, gaming, or spending time with my girlfriend. I am a firm believer in the power of lifelong academia and am always looking for the next complex problem to solve.
